Regulatory Advice

After the banking crisis of 2008 the regulatory requirements for banks grew significantly in terms of complexity and implementation costs.

We can help you to fulfill these regulatory requirements in a cost efficient way by leveraging our extensive experience with Basel III and FRTB programs in various major financial institutions.

Case Study 1: Back-Testing Policy for IMM Credit Risk Models

The Client's Requirements

The client, a global South African bank, planned to apply for IMM under Basel III. The IMM waiver required the institution to introduce a back-testing framework including principles, policies and procedures.

Our Solution

Based on our experience with an IMM application to the UK FSA, we helped the client to define a robust back-testing framework, which was practical and fulfilled the regulatory requirements.

The Client's Benefits

In addition to being able to meet the regulatory requirements, we provided to the firm a framework, which allows to quantify the quality of the model in economic terms. In addition to providing a pass/fail statement for the model performance, we were able to give to management a cash denominated estimate of the severity of the model's shortcomings.

Case Study 2: Fundamental Review of the Trading Book (FRTB)

The Client's Requirements

The Market Risk department of a major Scandinavian Bank decided to perform a feasibility study to assess the challenges for implementing the upcoming FRTB regulations.

Our Solution

We worked in partnership with a global management consulting firm to implement a FRTB compliant prototype of a market risk framework. As leader of the workstream for data, we identified gaps in the data collectoion, transformation and storage processes as well as problems with data quality. Based on this we issued some recommendations, how to close this gap and achieve compliance. Furthermore we initiated first mitigating measures, which will be the base of a strategic solution.

The Client's Benefits

The client has a better understanding of data-related risks and now has a plan how to mitigate the existing shortcomings in order to get FRTB compliant.
